# Lesson 3: Human vs. Robot Agency

## Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to articulate the key differences between human and robot agency and discuss the implications for designing safe and effective human-robot interactions.

## Prerequisites

- Completion of Lesson 2: Embodied Intelligence

## Concept Explanation

Agency refers to the capacity of an actor to act in a particular environment. Human agency is characterized by intention, free will, and a rich understanding of social and cultural context. Robot agency, on the other hand, is currently limited to the execution of pre-programmed behaviors or policies learned from data. Understanding this distinction is crucial for setting realistic expectations for robot capabilities and for designing systems where humans and robots can work together safely and productively.

## Real-World Analogy

Think about the difference between a human chef and a robotic arm in a factory that assembles sandwiches. The human chef can improvise, adapt to new ingredients, and understand the nuances of a customer's request. The robotic arm can only perform the specific sequence of actions it has been programmed to do. The human has a high degree of agency, while the robot has a very limited form of agency.

## Hands-On Task

- **Scenario Analysis**: Consider a scenario where a humanoid robot is assisting an elderly person at home. What are some of the potential risks and challenges that arise from the differences in human and robot agency? How might you design the robot's behavior to mitigate these risks?

## Common Mistakes & Debugging Tips

- **Anthropomorphism**: A common mistake is to attribute human-like intentions and understanding to robots. It's important to remember that a robot's "agency" is a product of its programming and does not imply consciousness or free will.
